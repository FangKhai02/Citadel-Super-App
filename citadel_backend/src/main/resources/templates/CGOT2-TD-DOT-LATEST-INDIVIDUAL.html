(The Settlor and the Trustee are hereinafter collectively referred to as “the Parties” and/or individually as “a Party”, as the context may permit)

WHEREAS:

  1. The Settlor is desirous of creating a trust for the benefit of the Beneficiary (as specified and defined in Clause 2.1 and under Schedule 1 of this Deed).

  2. The Trustee is a company incorporated in Malaysia under the laws of Malaysia and registered as a trust corporation under the Trust Companies Act 1949 and authorised to conduct any of the activities listed under Section 8 of the Trust Companies Act 1949.

  3. The Settlor wishes to establish a trust in favour of the Beneficiary and therefore is desirous of appointing the Trustee to act as a trustee to manage and administer the Trust Fund in accordance with the terms and conditions herein and the Trustee is agreeable to such appointment.

NOW IT IS AGREED BY THE PARTIES as follows: -

1.  Definition

1.1  The headings in this Deed are inserted for convenience only and shall not be taken, read and construed as essential parts of this Deed;
1.2  References to clauses unless otherwise stated are to be construed as references to clauses of this Deed;
1.3  All references to provisions of statutes include such provisions as modified or re-certified or re-enacted;
1.4  Words applicable to natural persons include any body of persons, company, corporation, firm or partnership, corporate or unincorporated and vice versa;
1.5  Words importing the masculine gender shall include the feminine and neuter gender and vice versa;
1.6  The Schedules form part of this Deed and shall have effect as if set out in full in the body of this Deed and any references to this Deed includes the Schedules.
1.7  Unless the context otherwise requires, words in the singular shall include the plural and vice versa;
1.8  Working days shall mean the working days in the Federal Territory of Wilayah Persekutuan Kuala Lumpur.

2.  Declaration of Trust

2.1  It is declared that the Settlor hereby establishes a revocable trust in favour of the person(s) listed in Schedule 1 (hereinafter collectively referred to as “the Beneficiary”) and appoints the Trustee as the trustee and the Trustee accepts such appointment.
2.2  The Settlor declares the following: -
(a)  that at no point in time has the Trustee promoted, persuaded or influenced the Settlor in any way on the establishment of this Trust; and
(b)  this Trust has been personally evaluated by the Settlor and the Settlor fully exonerates and discharges the Trustee of any liability arising from the establishment of this Trust.
2.3  The Settlor is not declared bankrupt and that no proceedings for insolvency has been commenced against the Settlor.
2.4  The Settlor hereby declares and represents that such declaration and representation shall form the basis of which the Trustee enters into this Deed.

3.  Purpose of the Trust

3.1  The purpose of this Trust is for the Trustee to manage the monies (“the Trust Fund”) as described in the Second Schedule of this Agreement and to provide the proceeds (if any) of the Trust Fund (“the Projected Return”) to the Beneficiary throughout the tenure of the Trust which shall be two (2) years from the date of this Trust Deed (“the Tenure”) the details of the Trust is specified in the Second Schedule hereof.
3.2  The terms of the Projected Return of the Authorised Investment as defined in Clause 5.2 herein shall be dependent on the terms of the respective investments made under Clause 5 and Clause 8 below.

4.  Beneficiary

4.1  The Beneficiary of this Trust shall be the person(s) identified in Schedule 1 of this Deed and shall be bound by the terms and conditions of this Deed during the Tenure of Trust and as long as they remain as Beneficiary.
4.2  Upon the instruction of the Settlor, the Trustee may at any time appoint additional person(s) as Beneficiary or remove any of the existing Beneficiary under this Trust by providing written notice to the Trustee and such change shall be effective within three (3) working days from the date the Trustee receives such notice.
4.3  In the event of death of any of the Beneficiary, if there is more than one Beneficiary, the share of such Beneficiary would be distributed equally between the remaining Beneficiary. In the event of death of the Beneficiary who is the only Beneficiary, then such shares will be distributed to the Settlor. As such, the death of any one of the Beneficiary shall not terminate this Trust or in any manner affect the powers of the Trustee.

5.  Management of the Trust Fund

5.1  The Settlor hereby fully authorises and grants a discretionary mandate for the Trustee to manage and administer the Trust Fund as provided in Clause 6 of this Deed of which the Trustee shall have all the powers over and in respect of the Trust Fund and the Trustee could exercise as if the Trustee is the owner of the Trust Fund to invest, in part or in whole, in the Authorised Investment.
5.2  Authorised Investments means any investment made by the Trustee at the instruction of the Settlor or the Settlor’s appointed agent (where applicable) to generate income and profits arising from the Trust Fund, including but not limited to deposit of the Trust Fund in any accounts opened with any financial institution licensed by the Central Bank of Malaysia, any investment in equity of any entities incorporated under the Companies Act 2016 and Limited Liability Partnership 2012 (or its predecessor Acts, where applicable), investment in any immovable property or any other type of investments that the Settlor or Settlor’s appointed agent, as the case may be, deem appropriate (“the Authorised Investment”).

6.  Trustee Capacity and Powers

6.1  Subject to the mandate of the Settlor as provided in Clause 5.1 of this Deed for the management and administration of the Trust Fund, the Trustee shall have full discretion in the management of the Trust Fund which shall include the following powers, which shall be in addition to all the powers conferred by the Trustee Act 1949:-
(a)  to invest the Trust Fund in the Authorised Investment;
(b)  to execute power of attorney or other necessary documents to any person for the purpose of executing, administering or managing the whole or any part of the Trust Fund in accordance to this Deed;
(c)  to appoint any one or more of its own officers as its attorney (jointly and severally if more than one) with power to execute documents on behalf of the Trustee to manage the day-to-day operation of the Trust;
(d)  upon consulting and obtaining an expert opinion, to initiate or to defend, at its discretion, any cause of action, affecting the Trust, whereupon all incidental legal fee and cost thereof shall be deducted from the Trust Fund.
(e)  To safeguard client’s assets as to ensure that the general principles and prescriptive requirements are consistently adhered.

7.  Disbursement of Trust Fund

7.1  In the event that the Trustee exercises its discretion conferred under Clause 5 and Clause 6 and subject to Clause 11 below, the Trustee shall disburse the Projected Return, if any, to the Beneficiary during the Tenure of this Trust commencing from the date of this Deed. If there are no Beneficiary, the Projected Return shall be released to the Settlor under the same terms of this Clause, and if the Settlor has passed away, the Projected Return to be released to the Settlor’s successor or heir, subject to the applicability of Clause 7.3 below.
7.2  Subject to Clause 11 below, the disbursement of the Projected Return, if any, shall be transferred and/or delivered to the recipient within fifteen (15) days or any extended period as mutually agreed between the parties from the date of receipt of the same by the Trustee, nett of all fee after the deduction of Trustee Fee hereunder. At the expiry and/or termination of the Trust, the Trustee shall release the Trust Fund and any accumulated income derived from the Trust Fund minus all liabilities, debt and fees owing to the Trustee, to the Beneficiary. If there are no Beneficiary, the Trust Fund shall be released to the Settlor under the same terms of this Clause.
7.3  In the event of the Settlor’s death prior to the expiry of the Tenure of Trust, the continuation of the Trust until its expiry shall be subject to the decision of the Beneficiary (in order of priority) or if there are no Beneficiary, the Settlor’s successor or heir. If it is decided for the discontinuation of the Trust, the Trust Fund and the Projected Return (if any) shall be distributed to the Beneficiary and must be released within seven (7) to ten (10) working days upon receiving full documents or if there are no Beneficiary, the Settlor’s successor or heir within one calendar month from the date of decision by the Beneficiary or if there are no Beneficiary, the Settlor’s successor or heir, as the case may be, or such longer duration of time as may be agreed. For the avoidance of doubt, for the purpose of this sub-clause, the Trust shall continue to operate until decided otherwise or disburse upon maturity of Trust unless decided otherwise by the Beneficiary.
7.4  Notwithstanding anything herein, the release of any Trust Fund and/or Projected Return shall be dependent on the provision of the satisfactory supporting documents to be provided by the Settlor and/or the Beneficiary and/or the successor or heir, as the case may be, as determined by the Trustee from time to time. For the avoidance of doubt, the Trustee shall not be liable or responsible for any damages caused by the delay in the release of the Trust Fund and/or the Projected Return as a result of the failure to comply with the terms of this Clause by the Settlor and/or the Beneficiary and/or the successor or heir, as the case may be.
7.5  Notwithstanding anything herein, prior to the release of the Trust Fund and/or any accumulated income derived from the Trust Fund, the sum may be subjected to costs and expenses directly or indirectly incurred in connection to the establishment, management, operation and administration of the Trust including but not limited to any tax liability and Trustee Fee, charges or assessments whatsoever, stamp duties, statutory goods and services tax or any other governmental taxes or charges payable by the Trustee (if any) for which the Trustee may be liable in respect of such distribution (hereinafter referred to as “the Trust Expenses”). For the avoidance of doubt, before making any distribution, the Trustee may deduct for the Trust Expenses from the Trust Fund and/or any accumulated income derived from the Trust Fund in accordance with Clause 12 below.

8.  Revocable Trust

8.1  The Trust hereby established shall be revocable.
8.2  In the event the Settlor wishes to terminate the Trust prior to the expiry of the Tenure of the Trust, the Settlor shall provide one (1) month prior written notice to the Trustee and provided always that all outstanding Trustee Fees have been fully settled. Notwithstanding any other clauses herein, the Trustee shall be entitled to terminate the Trust or resign as trustee of the Trust at any time by providing at least thirty (30) days prior written notice to the Settlor.

9.  Determination of Trust

9.1  This Trust shall be determined upon the full disbursement of the Trust Fund and the Projected Return (if any), in accordance with this Deed, to the Settlor or Beneficiary or the Settlor’s heir or successor, as the case may be, including but not limited to the expiry of the Tenure.
9.2  Early Redemption
(a)  The Settlor can exercise an early redemption of the Trust Fund at any time starting from the Second Year from the date of this Deed by providing one (1) month prior written notice to the Trustee which will be subject to a twenty-five per cent (25%) penalty on the Trust Fund placement (“Early Redemption Penalty”).
(b)  The early redemption price will be due and payable by the Trustee within two (2) months from the date of the acknowledgement of receipt of the said written notice by the Trustee.
(c)  In this instance, the Settlor shall be entitled to the Projected Return declared and/or to be declared (on a pro-rata basis) for the said one (1) months’ notice period given by the Settlor to the Company
(d)  There will be a cooling-off period of four (4) days for clients to revoke their trust funds upon CTB received the money.
(e)  For avoidance of doubt, early redemption by the Settlor is not allowed in the First Year of Tenure and redemption of the Settlor at the expiry of the Tenure shall not be subject to any early redemption penalty.

10.  Advocate and Solicitor, Advisor and Agent

10.1  The Trustee may in the conduct of the trust business employ and pay an agent whether a solicitor or other person, to transact or concur in doing all acts required to be done by the Trustee, including the receipt and payment of money, and any agent so employed shall be entitled to the relevant professional fees, charges and disbursement for business transacted and acts done by such agents, and the Trustee shall be fully reimbursed in respect of all costs so incurred and be entitled to set off such charges from the Trust Fund and/or any income derived from the Trust Fund.

11.  Trustee Fee

11.1  In consideration of the services provided by the Trustee, the Trustee may charge a fee for such amount as may be prescribed by the Trustee from time to time (“the Trustee Fee”) of which the Trustee is entitled to reimburse themselves the Trustee Fee from the Trust Fund.
11.2  The Settlor shall on presentation to him/her of the appropriate invoices, arrange or procure to arrange payment to or reimbursement of the Trustee Fees within fourteen (14) days from the date of such presentation in respect of all costs (including the Trustee Fee and legal costs), out of pocket expenses and other outgoings incurred by the Trustee in the performance of its duties hereunder including but not limited to all remuneration and other sums (which will be at normal commercial rates) payable to any agent, sub-agent or delegate appointed, if any.
11.3  For the avoidance of doubt, if the Settlor fails to pay the Trustee the Trustee Fee and/or the Trust Expenses, the Trustee shall be entitled to set-off the sums owing to them properly incurred from the performance of its duties from the Trust Fund and/or the Projected Return or from any accumulated income derived from the Trust Fund.
11.4  In the event that the Trust Fund is insufficient towards the settlement of the outstanding Trustee Fees when the same become due and payable, unless otherwise prohibited by law, the Trustee shall have lien over the Trust Fund with the power to sell any or all the assets under the Trust for the settlement of the outstanding Trustee Fee.

12.  Duties of Trustee

It shall be the Trustee’s duty to: -

(a)  to maintain a complete record of all the necessary details of the Beneficiary including the name and residence of the Beneficiary and such other details as the Trustee may deem fit;
(b)  to maintain accurate books and records showing the transactions i.e. receipts and disbursements relating to the Trust Fund and such other details as the Trustee may deem fit;
(c)  to maintain books of the Trust for inspection by the Beneficiary and/or Settlor upon request at such reasonable times at the main office of the Trustee;
(d)  to furnish the Beneficiary at special meetings the financial standing of the Trust and of such other accounts or information concerning the affairs of the Trust as the Beneficiary shall request.

13.  Indemnification

13.1  The Trustee shall be entitled to be fully and completely indemnified to its satisfaction out of the Trust Fund including its proceeds against any costs (including legal costs on a solicitor-client basis), expenses, claims, demands, actions, proceedings, penalties, charges, losses, damages and liabilities of whatever nature howsoever incurred by them in the execution and discharge of their duties or in relation to the Trust and the Trust Fund, save where the same is caused by the actual fraud, gross negligence, wilful default or breach of trust by the Trustee.
13.2  To the extent permissible by the law, this clause shall survive the determination of this Deed and continue for so long as is necessary to give its full effect, notwithstanding any other provision in this Deed.

14.  Personal Data

14.1  The Settlor hereby warrants and represents that the Settlor has procured the consent of the owners(s) (if applicable) of the personal data that is provided to the Trustee which may include the personal data of the Settlor, Administrator and/or the Beneficiary, as the case may be (hereinafter referred to as “the Personal Data”).
14.2  The Settlor unconditionally and irrevocably agrees that the Trustee may give, produce, divulge, reveal or otherwise make available the said Personal Data as may be necessary to any party in facilitating the execution of this Trust.
14.3  The Trustee undertakes not to transfer such Personal Data other than for the purpose of fulfilling its duties and to take reasonable measures to ensure that the Personal Data are stored safely and secure.

15.  Confidentiality

The parties agree that the contents of this Deed and all information provided by one party to another in connection with this Deed or in the course of negotiations of this Deed, except for such information which is or becomes part of the public domain through no act or omission of the either party, must be held in strict confidence by each party and its respective officers, employees, agents and servants and shall not without the prior written consent of such other party, divulge the Information to any other person or use the Information other than for carrying out the purposes of this Deed except to the extent that is required by law.

16.  Exclusion of Liability

16.1 

 In the execution of the Trust and the powers of the Trustee, the Trustee and the agents shall not be responsible or liable for any loss suffered by reason of any improper investment made at the instruction of the Settlor and/or the Settlor’s agent and/or from the carrying on of any business in good faith or in connection with the administration and management of the Trust by reason of any act or mistake made in good faith by the Trustee unless it is a result of gross negligence, wilful or fraudulent misconduct.
16.2  The Trustee shall be entitled to act upon the opinion or information obtained from legal counsel or other professionals whether instructed by the Trustee and/or the Settlor, and the Trustee shall be entitled to act upon any advice obtained from the bankers, accountants, valuers and other person appointed by the Trustee and believed by the Trustee to be an expert in relation to the matters upon which they are consulted whereupon the Trustee shall not be liable for any losses suffered by the parties in reliance upon such opinion, advice, statement or information provided by any such professionals.

17.  Solicitors’ Costs and Stamp Duty

17.1  All legal and/or solicitors’ costs in drawing up this Deed and/or any other supplementary or additional documents incidental hereto shall be borne by the Settlor which may be deducted from the Trust Fund, unless otherwise stated by the parties.
17.2  All stamp duty and registration fees thereon shall be paid by the Settlor which may be deducted from the Trust Fund. The Settlor further agrees to pay, as and when required any additional or excess stamp duty and/or penalty that may be imposed by the Collector of Stamp Duties or such other competent authorities in respect of this Deed.

18.  Notices

Any notice required to be served or made by either party hereto to the other shall be in writing and shall be deemed to be sufficiently served: -

(a)  if it is sent by prepaid registered post addressed to the other party at the address hereinbefore mentioned or to their last known address or to his solicitors and in such a case it shall be deemed (whether it is actually delivered or not) to have been received at the time when such registered letter would have, in the ordinary course, be delivered;
(b)  if it is despatched by hand to the other party or his solicitor upon due acknowledgement thereof;
(c)  if sent by courier addressed to the other party at the address hereinbefore mentioned or to their last known address or to his solicitors shall be deemed to have been received upon acknowledgement of receipt of the same; or
(d)  if sent by email, when the sender receives an automated message confirming delivery the correct documents and contents were emailed; or 30 minutes after the time sent (as recorded on the device from which the sender sent the email) unless the sender receives an automated message that the email has not been delivered, whichever happens first.

It is agreed between the parties as follows: -

(b)  Heading / Schedules

The First Schedule and the Second Schedule hereto shall be taken read and construed as an essential part of this Deed (if any).

(d)  Indemnity

(e)  General Duties of Parties

If any provision hereof is held to be illegal, invalid or unenforceable under present or future laws, such provision shall be fully severable and this Deed shall be construed and enforced as if such illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision had never comprised a part hereof and the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect and shall not be affected by the illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision or by its severance herefrom. In lieu of such illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision, there shall be added automatically as part hereof a provision, as similar in terms to such illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision as may be possible that is legal, valid and enforceable so as to give effect to the intent of the parties hereunder.
(h)  Successors ad Assigns

This Deed shall be binding on the respective heirs, personal representatives, successors in title and assigns of the parties.
(i)  No Assignment

Unless otherwise provided in this Deed, neither party shall assign or transfer all or any part of its rights under this Deed nor delegate its performance under this Deed without the prior written consent of the other party. Any assignment, transfer or delegation which is made without such prior written consent shall constitute a breach of this Deed.
(j)  No Partnership or Agency

The provisions of this Deed shall not be construed or taken to constitute (i) a partnership between the parties; (ii) either party to be the agent of the other party; or (iii) an authority to either party to represent or bind or pledge the credit of any of the other party in any way.
(k)  Entire Deed

This Deed shall herewith constitute the entire agreement between the Parties hereto with respect to the subject matter of this Deed and shall supersede all prior agreements and undertakings, both written and oral, with respect to the subject matter hereof except as otherwise expressly provided herein.
(l)  Rights and Remedies

The rights and remedies provided in this Deed are cumulative and are not exclusive of any rights or remedies of the parties provided at law, and no failure or delay in the exercise or the partial exercise of any such right or remedy or the exercise of any other right or remedy shall affect or impair any such right or remedy.
(m)  Counterparts

This Deed may be executed by any one or more of the parties hereto in any number of counterparts, each of which shall be deemed to be original, but all such counterparts shall together constitute the same instrument.
(n)  Governing Law and Jurisdiction

This Deed shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of Malaysia and the parties irrevocably submit to the jurisdiction of the Courts of Malaysia.

20.  Arbitration

(a)  Any unresolved dispute controversy or claim between the Parties shall be referred to and finally resolved by arbitration in Malaysia by an arbitrator to be agreed upon between the Parties or, failing agreement within fourteen (14) days after either Party has given to the other a written request to agree to the appointment of an arbitrator, a person to be nominated by the Director of the Asian International Arbitration Centre (AIAC) at the request of either Party.
(b)  The arbitration shall be in accordance with the Arbitration Act 2005 and the arbitration rules of the AIAC for the time being in force which rules are deemed to be incorporated by reference into this clause.
(c)  The AIAC arbitral tribunal shall consist of one arbitrator to be agreed upon between the Parties. Either Party may propose to the other the name or names of one or more persons, one of whom would serve as the sole arbitrator. If no agreement is reached within thirty (30) days after receipt by one Party of such a proposal from the other, the arbitrator shall be appointed by the Director of the AIAC.
(d)  All AIAC proceedings shall be conducted, including all documents presented in such proceedings, in the English language.
(e)  The arbitration award shall be final and binding on the Parties and judgement upon the award entered in arbitration may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

21.  Force Majeure

(a)  The Parties hereto shall not be liable for failures or delays in performing their obligations hereunder arising from any cause beyond their control, including but not limited to, act of God, acts of civil or military authority, fires, strikes, lockouts or labour disputes, pandemic/ epidemics, wars, riots, earthquakes, storms, typhoons and floods and in the event of any such delay, the time for either Party's performance shall be extended for a period equal to the time lost by reason of the delay. Save where such delay is caused by the act or omission of the other Party, in which event the rights, remedy and liabilities of the Parties shall be those conferred and imposed by the terms of this Deed and by law.
(b)  In the event of any foregoing delay occurs, the delaying Party shall promptly notify the other in writing of the reasons for the delay and the likely duration of the delay, whereby the performance of such Party’s obligation shall be suspended during the period that the conditions specified in the foregoing sub-clause persist and such Party shall be granted an extension of time for performance equal to the period of the delay. Provided that if the conditions shall continue beyond the duration of six (6) months, either Party may terminate this Deed forthwith by written notice to the other Party.
(c)  Upon such termination, the Trustee shall release the Trust Fund and any accumulated income (if any) derived from the Trust Fund minus all liabilities, debt and fees owing to the Trustee, to the Beneficiary and this Deed shall become null and void and be of no further effect and neither party shall have any further claims against the other arising from this Deed, save for antecedent breach if any.
