2.  Declaration of Trust

2.1  It is declared that the Settlor hereby establishes an irrevocable trust in favour of the person(s) listed in Schedule 1 (hereinafter collectively referred to as “the Beneficiary”) and appoints the Trustee as the trustee.
2.2  The Settlor and the Trustee shall be governed by the principle of Wadi’ah whereas the Settlor and the Beneficiary(s) shall be governed by the principle of Hibah.
2.3  The Settlor irrevocably placed the Trust Fund with the Trustee to administer and manage pursuant to Wadi’ah principle, subject to sub-Clause 7.4, Clauses 8, 9, 11 and 12 below and provided that Clause 6 and sub-Clauses 7.1, 7.2 and 7.3 below have been complied with.
2.4  By signing the Declaration of Trust and the Trust Deed, the Settlor and the Trustee has entered into a Trust agreement in accordance with the principle of Wadi’ah recognised by the Shariah Law and amounts to the offer and acceptance from both parties.
2.5  The Settlor declares the following: -
(a)  that at no point in time has the Trustee promoted, persuaded or influenced the Settlor in any way on the establishment of this Deed;
(b)  this Deed has been personally evaluated by the Settlor and the Settlor fully exonerates and discharges the Trustee of any liability arising from the establishment of this Deed;
(c)  that is a company duly incorporated and existing under the laws of Malaysia and it has not been placed under receivership and there are no steps being commenced or pending to place it under receivership;
(d)  that has not been wound up and is a going concern and there are no winding-up proceedings being commenced or pending against it;
(e)  that has not defaulted any agreement to which it is a party or by which it is bound, and no litigation, arbitration or administrative proceedings are presently current or pending or threatened which default, litigation, arbitration or administrative proceedings, as the case may be, might materially affect its ability to enter into and/or to perform its obligations under this Deed;
(f)  that shall obtain all necessary approvals including but not limited to any form of contractual and/or regulatory approval(s) from any relevant regulatory bodies and/or persons for the time being having jurisdiction over the subject-matter in respect of this Deed
(g)  that it has full power, authority and legal capacity to execute, deliver and perform the obligations under this Deed;
(h)  that all information furnished by the Settlor in connection with this Deed, does not contain any untrue statement or omit to state any fact the omission of which makes any statements made therein in the light of the circumstances under which they are made, misleading, and are made on reasonable grounds after due and careful inquiry and it is not aware of any material facts or circumstances that have not been disclosed to the other party which might, if disclosed, adversely affect the decision of a person considering whether or not to enter into this Deed; and
(i)  that this Deed constitutes the valid and binding obligations of the party and enforceable in accordance with its terms.
2.6  The Settlor hereby declares and represents that such declaration and representation shall form the basis of which the Trustee enters into this Deed.

3.  Purpose of the Trust

3.1  The purpose of this Deed is for the Trustee to manage and administer the Trust Fund and to provide the proceeds (if any) from the Trust Fund to the Beneficiary in accordance with Clause 7 below, throughout the tenure of the Trust as specified in Schedule 2 hereof.
3.2  The Trustee is hereby authorised by the Settlor to utilize the Trust Fund in accordance with the terms of the Authorised Placement as defined in Clause 5 below. For the avoidance of doubt, the Trustee shall not be held liable and/or responsible for the delay and/or non-payment of the proceeds of the Trust Fund (if any) as a result of the delay and/or failure by a third party or for reasons beyond its control.

4.  Beneficiary

4.1  The Beneficiary of this Trust shall be the person(s) identified in Schedule 1 of this Deed and shall be bound by the terms and conditions of this Deed during the Tenure of Trust and as long as they remain as Beneficiary.
4.2  Upon the instruction of the Settlor, the Trustee may at any time appoint additional person(s) as Beneficiary or remove any of the existing Beneficiary under this Trust by providing written notice to the Trustee and such change shall be effective within three (3) working days from the date the Trustee receives such notice.
4.3  In the event of death of any of the Beneficiary, if there is more than one Beneficiary, the share of such Beneficiary would be distributed equally between the remaining Beneficiary. In the event of death of the Beneficiary who is the only Beneficiary, then such shares will be distributed to the Settlor. As such, the death of any one of the Beneficiary shall not terminate this Trust or in any manner affect the powers of the Trustee.

5.  Management of the Trust Fund

5.1  The Settlor hereby fully authorises and grants a discretionary mandate for the Trustee to manage and administer the Trust Fund as provided in Clause 6 of this Deed of which the Trustee shall have all the powers over and in respect of the Trust Fund and the Trustee could exercise as if the Trustee is the owner of the Trust Fund to invest, in part or in whole, in the Authorised Investment.
5.2  Authorised Investments means any investment made by the Trustee at the instruction of the Settlor or the Settlor’s appointed agent (where applicable) to generate income and profits arising from the Trust Fund, including but not limited to deposit of the Trust Fund in any accounts opened with any financial institution licensed by the Central Bank of Malaysia, any investment in equity of any entities incorporated under the Companies Act 2016 and Limited Liability Partnership 2012 (or its predecessor Acts, where applicable), investment in any immovable property or any other type of investments that the Settlor or Settlor’s appointed agent, as the case may be, deem appropriate (“the Authorised Investment”).

6.  Trustee Capacity and Powers

6.1  Subject to the mandate of the Settlor as provided in Clause 5.1 of this Deed for the management and administration of the Trust Fund, the Trustee shall have the capacity and power in addition to all the powers conferred by the Trustee Act 1949 and Trust Companies 1949 as follows:-
(a)  to place the Trust Fund in the Authorised Placement;
(b)  to execute power of attorney or other necessary documents to any person for the purpose of executing, administering or managing the whole or any part of the Trust Fund in accordance to this Deed;
(c)  to appoint any one or more of its own officers as its attorney (jointly and severally if more than one) with power to execute documents on behalf of the Trustee to manage the day-to-day operation of the Trust;
(d)  upon consulting and obtaining an expert opinion, to initiate or to defend, at its discretion, any cause of action, affecting the Trust, whereupon all incidental legal fee and cost thereof shall be deducted from the Trust Fund;
(e)  to safeguard Settlor’s Trust Fund as to ensure that the general principles and prescriptive requirements are consistently adhered to; and
(f)  to hold and continue to hold on trust the Trust Fund on behalf of the Settlor, so long as they deem proper, and to place and replace in placements deemed Shariah-compliant by the Shariah Adviser as per the AAOIFI BNM and SCM Shariah Standards, based on Mudarabah, Musharakah, Wakalah, and/or other Shariah-compliant structures as approved by the Shariah Adviser.

7.  Disbursement of Trust Fund

7.1  In the event that the Trustee exercises its power under Clause 5 and Clause 6 above, the Trustee shall disburse the proceeds of the Trust Fund, if any, to the Beneficiary (in accordance to the order of priority), during the tenure of this Deed as specified in Schedule 2 hereof commencing from the date of this Deed. If there are no Beneficiary, the proceeds of the Trust Fund shall be released to the Settlor under the same terms of this Clause, and if the Settlor has become dissolved, the proceeds of the Trust Fund shall be released to the Settlor’s official receiver duly appointed by a Court of competent jurisdiction or by the Malaysian Department of Insolvency, subject to the applicability of Clause 7.4 below.
7.2  Subject to Clause 7.1 above, the disbursement of the proceeds of the Trust Fund, if any, shall be transferred and/or delivered to the recipient within thirty (30) working days or any extended period as mutually agreed between the Parties from the date of receipt of the same by the Trustee.
7.3  At the expiry and/or termination of the Trust, the Trustee shall release the Trust Fund and any accumulated earnings derived from the Trust Fund less accrued liabilities, debt and fees owing to the Trustee, to the Beneficiary. If there are no Beneficiary, the Trust Fund shall be released to the Settlor under the same terms of this Clause.
7.4  In the event of the Settlor become dissolved prior to the expiry of the tenure of Trust as specified in Schedule 2 hereof, the continuation of the remaining tenure of Trust as specified in Schedule 2 hereof shall be subject to the collective majority decision of the Beneficiary or if there are no Beneficiary, the Settlor’s official receiver upon his appointment by the Court of competent jurisdiction or by the Malaysian Department of Insolvency (as the case may be).
7.5  Whereby, upon receipt of notice to discontinue the Trust, the Trust Fund and the proceeds of the Trust Fund (if any) shall be distributed to the Beneficiary or if there are no Beneficiary, the Settlor’s official receiver within one (1) calendar month from the date of receipt of the said notice from the Beneficiary or if there are no Beneficiary, the Settlor’s official receiver duly appointed by a Court of competent jurisdiction or by the Malaysian Department of Insolvency, as the case may be, or such other duration as Parties may be agreed in writing. For the avoidance of doubt, for the purpose of this sub-clause, the Trust shall continue to operate until decided otherwise.
7.6  Notwithstanding anything herein, the release of any Trust Fund and the proceeds of the Trust Fund (if any) shall be dependent on the provision of the satisfactory supporting documents to be provided by the Settlor and/or the Beneficiary and/or the official receiver, to the Trustee for its verification and validation, as the case may be, as determined by the Trustee from time to time. For the avoidance of doubt, the Trustee shall not be liable or responsible for any damages caused by the delay in the release of the Trust Fund and the proceeds of the Trust Fund (if any) as a result of non-compliance with the terms of this Clause by the Settlor and/or the Beneficiary and/or the official receiver, as the case may be.
7.7  For avoidance of doubt, prior to the release of the Trust Fund and the proceeds of the Trust Fund (if any) pursuant to Clauses 7.1, 7.2, 7.3, 7.4 and 7.5 above, the sum may be subjected to costs and expenses directly or indirectly incurred in connection to the establishment, management, operation and administration of the Trust including but not limited to any tax liability and Trustee Fee pursuant to Clause 11 below, charges or assessments whatsoever, stamp duties, statutory goods and services tax or any other governmental taxes or charges payable by the Trustee (if any) for which the Trustee may be liable in respect of such distribution (hereinafter referred to as “the Trust Expenses”) which may be deducted from the Trust Fund and proceeds of the Trust Fund.

8.  Revocable Trust

8.1  The Trust hereby established shall be revocable at the option of the Settlor.
8.2  In the event the Settlor wishes to terminate the Trust prior to the expiry of the tenure of the Trust as specified in Schedule 2 hereof, the Settlor shall provide one (1) month prior written notice to the Trustee and provided always that all outstanding Trustee Fees and Trust Expenses have been fully settled.
8.3  Notwithstanding any other clauses herein, the Trustee shall be entitled to terminate the Trust or resign as trustee of the Trust at any time by providing at least one (1) month prior written notice to the Settlor.

9.  Determination of Trust

9.1  This Deed shall be determined upon the full disbursement of the Trust Fund and the proceeds of the Trust Fund (if any), in accordance with this Deed, to the Settlor or Beneficiary or the Settlor’s official receiver, as the case may be, including but not limited to the expiry of the tenure of Trust as specified in Schedule 2 hereof.
9.2  Prior to the expiry of the tenure of Trust as specified in Schedule 2 hereof, the same may be extended upon mutual consent by the Parties for a further duration as may be agreed in writing between the Parties.
9.3  Upon the disbursement/release of the Trust Fund and its proceeds (if any) in accordance with Clause 6 and Clause 7 above as the case may be, the Parties acknowledges and agree that the Trustee shall be effectively discharged from its duties and obligations as stipulated herein and no rights or claims shall thereafter be pursued against the Trustee arising from this Deed.
9.4  In the event the Settlor terminate this Deed at any stage of the Duration of Trust due to any reason whatsoever, the Trustee shall return the Trust Fund that is under the management of the Trustee to the Settlor or in the case of the dissolution of the Settlor, the proceeds of the Trust Fund shall be released to the Settlor’s official receiver, subject to the applicability of sub-Clause 7.4 above, as the case may be, subject to sub-Clause 7.7 and Clause 11.
9.5  The Trustee will return the Trust Fund to the Settlor within one (1) month after the issuance of the notice of acceptance of the said termination to the Settlor (if applicable) and provided that all outstanding Trustee Fees and Trust Expenses have been fully settled by the Settlor to the Trustee.
9.6  Subject to Clause 7 above, the distribution of Trust Fund where Beneficiary(s) are below the age of majority shall be effected upon attaining the age of eighteen (18).

10.  Advocate and Solicitor, Advisor and Agent

10.1 The Trustee may in the conduct of the trust business employ and pay an advisor and/or agent whether a solicitor or other person, to transact or concur in doing all acts required to be done by the Trustee, including the receipt and payment of money, and any advisor and/or agent so employed shall be entitled to the relevant professional fees, charges and disbursement for business transacted and acts done by such advisors and/or agents, and the Trustee shall be fully reimbursed in respect of all costs so incurred and be entitled to set off such charges from the Trust Fund and/or any earnings derived from the Trust Fund.

11.  Trustee Fee

11.1  In consideration of the services provided by the Trustee, the Trustee may charge a fee for such amount as may be prescribed by the Trustee from time to time (“the Trustee Fee”) of which the Trustee is entitled to reimburse themselves the Trustee Fee from the Trust Fund and/or proceeds of the Trust Fund
11.2  The Settlor shall on presentation to him/her of the appropriate invoices, arrange or procure to arrange payment to or reimbursement of the Trustee Fees within fourteen (14) days from the date of such presentation in respect of all costs (including the Trustee Fee and legal costs), out of pocket expenses and other outgoings incurred by the Trustee in the performance of its duties hereunder including but not limited to all remuneration and other sums (which will be at normal commercial rates) payable to any agent, sub-agent or delegate appointed, if any.
11.3  For the avoidance of doubt, if the Settlor fails to pay the Trustee the Trustee Fee and/or the Trust Expenses, the Trustee shall be entitled to set-off the sums owing to them properly incurred from the performance of its duties from the Trust Fund and/or the profit-sharing or from any accumulated earnings derived from the Trust Fund.
11.4  In the event that the Trust Fund is insufficient towards the settlement of the outstanding Trustee Fees when the same become due and payable, unless otherwise prohibited by law, the Trustee shall have lien over the Trust Fund with the power to sell any or all the assets under the Trust for the settlement of the outstanding Trustee Fee.

12.  Duties of Trustee

It shall be the Trustee’s duty to: -

(a)  to maintain a complete record of all the necessary details of the Beneficiary including the name and residence of the Beneficiary and such other details as the Trustee may deem fit;
(b)  to maintain accurate books and records showing the transactions i.e. receipts and disbursements relating to the Trust Fund and such other details as the Trustee may deem fit;
(c)  to maintain books of the Trust for inspection by the Beneficiary and/or Settlor upon request at such reasonable times at the main office of the Trustee;
(d)  to furnish the Beneficiary at special meetings the financial standing of the Trust and of such other accounts or information concerning the affairs of the Trust as the Beneficiary shall request.

13.  Indemnification

13.1  The Trustee shall be entitled to be fully and completely indemnified to its satisfaction out of the Trust Fund including its proceeds against any costs (including legal costs on a solicitor-client basis), expenses, claims, demands, actions, proceedings, penalties, charges, losses, damages and liabilities of whatever nature howsoever incurred by them in the execution and discharge of their duties or in relation to the Trust and the Trust Fund, save where the same is caused by the actual fraud, gross negligence, wilful default or breach of trust by the Trustee.
13.2  To the extent permissible by the law, this clause shall survive the determination of this Deed and continue for so long as is necessary to give its full effect, notwithstanding any other provision in this Deed.

14.  Exclusion of Liability

14.1 

In addition to Clause 13.1 above, in the execution of the Trust and the powers of the Trustee, the Trustee and its representative(s) shall not be responsible or liable for:-

a) any loss suffered by reason of any improper placement made at the instruction of the Settlor and/or the Settlor’s representative; and/or

b) from the carrying on of any business in good faith or in connection with the administration and management of the Trust;

by reason of any act or mistake made in good faith by the Trustee unless it is a result of gross negligence, wilful or fraudulent misconduct.
14.2  The Trustee shall be entitled to act upon the opinion or information obtained from legal counsel or other professionals whether instructed by the Trustee and/or the Settlor, and the Trustee shall be entitled to act upon any advice obtained from the bankers, accountants, valuers and other person appointed by the Trustee and believed by the Trustee to be an expert in relation to the matters upon which they are consulted whereupon the Trustee shall not be liable for any losses suffered by the Parties in reliance upon such opinion, advice, statement or information provided by any such professionals.

15.  Personal Data

15.1  The Settlor hereby warrants and represents that the Settlor has procured the consent of the owners(s) (if applicable) of the personal data that is provided to the Trustee which may include the personal data of the Settlor, Administrator and/or the Beneficiary, as the case may be (hereinafter referred to as “the Personal Data”).
15.2  The Settlor unconditionally and irrevocably agrees that the Trustee may give, produce, divulge, reveal or otherwise make available the said Personal Data as may be necessary to any party in facilitating the execution of this Deed.
15.3  The Trustee undertakes not to transfer such Personal Data other than for the purpose of fulfilling its duties and to take reasonable measures to ensure that the Personal Data are stored safely and secure.

16.  Confidentiality

The Parties agree that the contents of this Deed and all information provided by one party to another in connection with this Deed or in the course of negotiations of this Deed, except for such information which is or becomes part of the public domain through no act or omission of the either party, must be held in strict confidence by each party and its respective officers, employees, agents and servants and shall not without the prior written consent of such other party, divulge the Information to any other person or use the Information other than for carrying out the purposes of this Deed except to the extent that is required by law.

17.  Solicitors’ Costs and Stamp Duty

17.1  All legal and/or solicitors’ costs in drawing up this Deed and/or any other supplementary or additional documents incidental hereto shall be borne by the Settlor which may be deducted from the Trust Fund, unless otherwise stated by the parties.
17.2  All stamp duty and registration fees thereon shall be paid by the Settlor which may be deducted from the Trust Fund. The Settlor further agrees to pay, as and when required any additional or excess stamp duty and/or penalty that may be imposed by the Collector of Stamp Duties or such other competent authorities in respect of this Deed.

18.  Notices

Any notice required to be served or made by either party hereto to the other shall be in writing and shall be deemed to be sufficiently served: -

(a)  if it is sent by prepaid registered post addressed to the other party at the address hereinbefore mentioned or to their last known address or to his solicitors and in such a case it shall be deemed (whether it is actually delivered or not) to have been received at the time when such registered letter would have, in the ordinary course, be delivered;
(b)  if it is despatched by hand to the other party or his solicitor upon due acknowledgement thereof;
(c)  if sent by courier addressed to the other party at the address hereinbefore mentioned or to their last known address or to his solicitors shall be deemed to have been received upon acknowledgement of receipt of the same; or
(d)  if sent by email, when the sender receives an automated message confirming delivery the correct documents and contents were emailed; or thirty (30) minutes after the time sent (as recorded on the device from which the sender sent the email) unless the sender receives an automated message that the email has not been delivered, whichever happens first.

19.  Miscellaneous

It is agreed between the parties as follows: -

(a)  Time is of Essence

Time wherever mentioned shall be of the essence of this Deed, both as regards the dates and periods specifically mentioned and as to any substituted dates and periods which may be agreed in writing between the parties.
(b)  Heading / Schedules

The First Schedule and the Second Schedule hereto shall be taken read and construed as an essential part of this Deed (if any).
(c)  No Waiver

Knowledge or acquiescence by either party hereto of any breach of any of the conditions or covenants herein contained shall not operate as or be deemed to be waiver of such conditions or covenants or any of them and notwithstanding such knowledge or acquiescence each party hereto shall be entitled to exercise their respective rights under this Deed and to require strict performance by the other of the terms and conditions herein.
(d)  Indemnity

Any indemnity contained in this Deed shall continue to have effect notwithstanding completion of this Deed herein.
(e)  General Duties of Parties

Each of the parties hereby agrees that he will upon the request of the other party hereto execute and do all such deeds, documents and things as may be necessary to carry this Deed into effect.
(f)  Variation of Terms

It is hereby agreed and declared by both parties that notwithstanding any of the provisions of this Deed to the contrary, the provisions and terms of this Deed may at any time and from time to time be varied or amended by mutual consent of the parties hereto by means of a mutual exchange of letters as the parties hereto may agree upon from time to time and thereupon such amendments and variations shall be deemed to become effective to the relevant provisions of this Deed and the relevant provisions of this Deed shall be deemed to have been amended or varied accordingly and shall be read and construed as if such amendments and variations have been incorporated in and had formed part of this Deed at the time of execution thereof.
(g)  Severability

If any provision hereof is held to be illegal, invalid or unenforceable under present or future laws, such provision shall be fully severable and this Deed shall be construed and enforced as if such illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision had never comprised a part hereof and the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect and shall not be affected by the illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision or by its severance herefrom. In lieu of such illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision, there shall be added automatically as part hereof a provision, as similar in terms to such illegal, invalid or unenforceable provision as may be possible that is legal, valid and enforceable so as to give effect to the intent of the parties hereunder.
(h)  Successors ad Assigns

This Deed shall be binding on the respective heirs, personal representatives, successors in title and assigns of the parties.
